<h4>The Archeometric Planisphere</h4>

<p><i>An organic, harmonic, and symmetrical instrument, founded upon the combination of several Zones of Circles, Radii, and concentric Polygons capable of evolving around a common central point.</i></p>

<p>The said Zones comprise:</p>

<p>1° A dual Circle of <b>360°</b> each evolving in opposite directions, such that each degree yields two numbers, their sum always being <b>360</b>, and their dextrogyral or levogyral inversion permitting easy metrology in both directions;</p>

<p><b>2° A fixed</b> duodenary Zone called <b>Zodiac of modal Letters</b>. It is divided into equal parts, each of <b>30°</b>, further divisible into minutes and seconds. Each twelfth of this <b>Zone</b> contains, at its center, its morphological letter and the number of that letter within an Escutcheon bearing a precisely corresponding special color;</p>

<p><b>Total: XII morphological Letters, XII arithmological Numbers, each specific to one of them, XII Colors;</b></p>

<p><b>3° A mobile</b> Zone called <b>planetary of Letters</b>.</p>

<p>This is formed by <b>XII Angles</b> of <b>IV equilateral Triangles</b> intersecting regularly. Each of the <b>XII Angles</b> is tangent to one of the <b>Escutcheons</b> of the preceding <b>Zone</b> and bears a morphological Letter, the arithmological Number proper to this Letter, the Color of the Escutcheon to which it is tangent, a musical staff, and a Note.</p>

<p><b>Total: XII Angles, XII Letters, XII Numbers, XII Colors, XII Notes;</b></p>

<p><b>4° A fixed</b> astral Zodiacal Zone, with its <b>XII Signs</b> derived from the <b>XII Zodiacal Letters</b>. Each Sign in its Escutcheon bearing the Color of its Correspondence with the <b>Zones</b> above;</p>

<p><b>5° A mobile</b> astral planetary Zone, with its <b>VII astral diatonic Signs</b> and all their Correspondences, each appearing in its proper Domiciles both Diurnal and Nocturnal, and bearing the Color of the planetary Letter from which its morphology derives.</p>

<p><b>Total: XII planetary Signs, of which V are repeated = VII, and XII Colors;</b></p>

<p><b>6° A Zone</b> of <b>XII angles</b> of <b>IV equilateral Triangles</b>, intersecting regularly beneath the <b>generative and metrological Triangle</b>, corresponding, by its angles, to the <b>Sign of Virgo</b>, the <b>Sign of Capricorn</b>, and the <b>Sign of Taurus</b>. Each Angle bears the Colors of the Sign to which it corresponds.</p>

<p><b>Total: XII Colors, XII Angles, IV Triangles including one generative, two hexagons or two Pairs of equilateral Tangent Triangles, one Pair solstitial by its North-South Angles, the other equinoctial by its East-West Angles, all Angles of each Pair spaced apart by the length of the Radius of their tangent Circle;</b></p>

<p><b>7° A central Circle</b> containing a <b>Musical Staff</b>, a <b>Note</b> at the Common Center, a morphological <b>Letter</b> on this Note, <b>XII white rays</b> forming <b>VI white diameters</b>, passing through the Center, one North-South, the other East-West, and all at <b>30°</b> from each other on the circle.</p>

<p>The organic and harmonic <b>planisphere</b> has its Poles and Equator of Correspondences, its year, its seasons, its left-right symmetry of organicities and harmonicities either direct or interferential. It speaks precisely through all its concordant elements and through all their combinations.</p>

<p>The analysis of its synthesis and the laws that presided over its composition will be given after its detailed description.</p>

<h4>Detailed Description</h4>

<h5>The Archeometer and Architechny</h5>

<p>The Archeometer is thus the precision instrument of higher sciences and their corresponding arts, their cosmometric protractor, their cosmological standard, their regulator and homological revealer.</p>

<p>It brings them back to their single universal principle, to their mutual concordance and to their synarchic synthesis.</p>

<p>This synthesis, which is none other than the genesis of the principle, is the very Word, and it autographs its own name upon the first triangle of the Archeometer: <i>SOPh-Ya</i>, Wisdom of God.</p>

<p>But to make understood all possible applications of the Archeometer as experimental revealer and regulator of this genesis and synthesis would require entering into endless developments.</p>

<p>It would require inventorying the entire nomenclature of the quadruple hierarchy of substances, facts, and consequently of divine, angelic, human and natural sciences and arts.</p>

<p>Moreover, it would require indicating, while entering into the universality of consequences, all equivalences and all correspondences of these hierarchies.</p>

<p>We shall reach the same demonstrative goal by a swifter path&mdash;that of fact alone, of experience in all its scientific truth and consequently in all the conscientious integrity one must demand of oneself in so grave a matter.</p>

<p>This fact is art&mdash;but art considered as the creative and conscious utterance of science itself, no longer as the individualist manifestation of anarchy, fancy, fashion or imitation.</p>

<p>This is why we concentrate the Archeometer's applications upon that Art which may become the most direct expression of Science returned to its principle&mdash;upon the art that synthesizes all sciences in action, all arts, all crafts and industries&mdash;in a word, the entire hierarchy of human labor.</p>

<p>This art is Architechny.</p>

<p>The masterwork of Architechny being the Religious Edifice&mdash;summary of the Principle of Law and of social fact&mdash;we shall apply the Archeometer to the science of religions.</p>

<p>Being potentially required to erect Christian cathedrals, Brahmanic pagodas<sup><a href="#T-31" id="ref-T-31">[&#42;]</a></sup>, Buddhist or Chinese temples, Zoroastrian fire temples or Muslim mosques, the architect will see upon the Archeometric Regulator the exact position of each of these religions within Religion&mdash;this word being used in its sense of scientific Synthesis and of Wisdom in the ancient meaning of the term.</p>

<p>The Archeometer will experimentally prove itself to be both Revealer and Regulator of Higher Studies&mdash;the Revealer of the Revelation foretold at the beginning of the 19<sup>th</sup> century by the Count de Maistre.</p>

<p>The Archeometric Principle being the Utterance, the architect will first use the double zone of letters, and this will simultaneously provide him with all its equivalences in sonometric numbers, colors, notes, musical modes, and consequently morphological forms.</p>

<p>He will only need to pass from the World of Glory to that of the astral Heavens to obtain the cosmological correspondences resulting from the preceding angelic and divine ones.</p>

<h5>Crown of 360 degrees or 36 decans</h5>

<p>The number 36, in decimal Sanskrit letters, is written: GO.</p>

<p>In the Veda, it signifies Heaven itself. The number 360, in Adamic numerical letters, is written: <i>ShaS</i>. It signifies the sexennial power 6&nbsp;&times;&nbsp;6&mdash;that which precisely corresponds to the measurement of the circle by the hexagon.</p>

<p>We shall later see the importance of this relation to the very Principle. The latter has, as its characteristic, the Trinity determining its direct instrumentality in sexenniality: 3...6; the equilateral trigone determining the hexagonal star.</p>

<p>This double circular protractor with inverse progression, regarding degrees, has a function of considerable practical verification&mdash;as facts will later attest&mdash;particularly concerning colors, the luminous Equivalents of the Utterance: Rays and colors.</p>

<p>Let us recall here, for the record, that the Gospel of Saint John, read in Aramaic Syriac, states: "The Principle is the Utterance, the Word." In patriarchal Greece&mdash;or rather in Slavic Balkans&mdash;Orpheus, according to traditions collected by the Church Fathers, had left among his numerous canonical books a work entitled: <i>The Utterance or the Sacred Word</i>. Likewise in patriarchal Italy, that of the Etruscans.</p>

<p>One need only explore antiquity somewhat across all parts of the world to find certain traces of the importance of human Utterance, considered as a reflection of the divine Word.</p>

<p>Undoubtedly, from India to China, from Slavdom and Scandinavia to ancient America, from Syria and Chaldea to Egypt, erudition can only reach the superstitious and magical remnants of the ancient science of this primordial Utterance and its alphabets.</p>

<p>Yet these very relics bear witness to this lost science.</p>

<h4>The Alphabets</h4>

<p>Among ancient alphabets predating the anarchistic Greco-Latin civilizations, we classify those with 22 numeral letters as typical equivalents of the Utterance.</p>

<p>It is through forgetting this distinction&mdash;taking effect for cause, consequence for Principle&mdash;that certain ancient Universities fell into the worship of astral Powers, Angels and demons; into Sabaeanism, even into fetishism; and that, a century ago, Dupuis plunged lower still into the crudest astronomical materialism.</p>

<p>We classify as Lunar the alphabets of 28 letters, as Zodiacal Horary those of 24, as Zodiacal Monthly those of 30, as Decanic those of 36, etc.&mdash;always under the aforementioned reservations, and relating all these numbers to XXII as the Standard.</p>

<p>Consequently, the cosmological function and position of each letter are determined by its formal kinship with the astral signs, whose placement is itself determined astronomically.</p>

<p>It follows that since letters position themselves thus and not by human hand, their placement, their binary and ternary groupings, all their interrelations are autological rather than anthropological. <p>So that when the resemblance between the Archeometric letter and its corresponding Zodiacal sign leaves some visual uncertainty, this is dispelled by the analogous letter in other alphabets&mdash;principally the Samaritan.</p>

<p>His beloved disciple Saint John begins his Gospel in Syriac Aramaic: "The Principle is the Word, and the Word is the ATH of the ALaHIM."</p>

<p>Thus he confirms and comments upon Moses, and shortly thereafter, he recalls the criterion of certitude by saying: "Life was the Light of Men." Thus the numerical root&mdash;or rather the only-begotten Son, the intimate asymmetry of the name I&Eacute;V&Eacute;&mdash;is KaVi, the creative Word; Agni, the divine Fire, that of absolute Life&mdash;Love, the Fire of Self-Sacrifice, universal Priest and Victim, Creator and Preserver, Redeemer and Savior.</p>

<p>Such is the Barashith, the Master and Lord of the Work of the 6 days.</p>

<p>This work is not directed, either, by the ALaHIM, the object and not the subject of the verb Bara. The Father's name is uttered only when living organicity and harmonicity are deployed in act within the eternal World of Glory and then in the temporal World of the astral Heavens, born from the amorphous nebula of chaos.</p>

<p>In the absolute Being, the Principle is to act what lightning is to thunder, what love is to the gift of Self.</p>

<p>There is no abstraction in God; all there is living, present, and indivisible. He is the Principle and the act.</p>

<p>Only the unconscious&mdash;philosophers renouncing the criterion of Life, the generative Principle, for the mental castration of Cogito ergo sum&mdash;could fashion God in the image of their impotence by separating principle from act, or rather by confounding the divine Principle with so-called natural origin.</p>

<p>Had the universal Principle not been the Realizer and primordial Reality of the eternal World of Glory&mdash;had realization been subjected to a secondary power like Time&mdash;this Principle would be dead, and not only would no life renew itself, but none would ever have manifested.</p>

<p>Now, the miracle of universal Life is the act of the living Principle&mdash;a fact as undeniable as it is inexplicable without God.</p>

<p>One might ask why the name I&Eacute;V&Eacute; is pronounced on the seventh day. The number 7 on the sonic string is always a number of rest.</p>

<p>The number 6 yields the so-called minor perfect chord, which I term internal organic, plus two fifths in the two following octaves.</p>

<p>I indicate here just one of the many experimental reasons demonstrating why the act of the creative Principle&mdash;whether theogonic in glory or cosmogonic in the astral heavens&mdash;is governed by the hexad.</p>

<p>Its repose lies at its center or that of the hexagon. "God has placed His throne in the sun," says the Prophet, speaking of the Sun of Glory, the Center of His hexad and double hexad.</p>

<p>In all that precedes, nothing has been uttered that was not the expression of an experimental archeometric fact&mdash;this being the honest character of science&mdash;and not of an arrogant and vain metaphysics.</p>

<p>The path of science, which is that of Truth, is at the same time the path of human humility&mdash;where the human spirit lets the law speak through fact, and the Principle through law.</p>

<p>It is the <i>Lavabo inter justos manus</i>. "I will wash my hands among the righteous"; and to be just before the universal facts that are signs of the Word, it suffices to be exact in their observation.</p>

<p>Science says with Jesus: <i>Fiat voluntas tua</i>&mdash;before the Author of facts submitted to its experimentation.</p>

<p>The philosopher, the mentally unconscious, always says, like the so-called qabalistic magician, "<i>Fiat voluntas mea</i>," and nothing results but void.</p>

<p>Compared to those of science, the social products of art in this age are of pitiful poverty when not tainted by an execrable perversity and perversion.</p>

<p>At the pinnacle of all arts, architechny drags itself through sterility&mdash;towed along by all productive epochs&mdash;unable to generate any style or genre.</p>

<p>The reason for its impotence is the same as for philosophers, metaphysicians, jurists, and literati&mdash;apes and parrots of Graeco-Latin pagans. It is unconsciousness&mdash;the absence of a scientific principle demonstrable scientifically, experimentally, honestly.</p>

<p>Faced with the anarchy currently reigning in all things, thanks to this mental race, some have dared to declare: "The bankruptcy of science."</p>

<p>No&mdash;science never goes bankrupt, for it is consciousness: an exact reflection of the Word's Incidence.</p>

<p>This dual light is instrumental to Life, just as Instruction is to Education or ought to be.</p>

<p>But bankruptcy of a mental race&mdash;yes; bankruptcy of whim and individual anarchy; bankruptcy of Graeco-Latin secondary studies&mdash;neo-pagan instruction without education.</p>

<h4>Summary Description</h4>

<p><i>from Circumference to Center:</i></p>

<h5>Crown of 360 Degrees&mdash;Differential Numerical Protractor</h5>

<p>The first two circumferences, divided into 360 degrees and evolving by contrary motion, yield 360 through addition at any given degree. This homology enables dual verification of all positions of other constituent parts.</p>

<p>These two circumferences form a Differential Numerical Protractor.</p>

<h5>Zodiacal Crown of the Utterance</h5>

<p>The second zone is tinted orange-pink. This is the Zodiac of the Utterance.</p>

<p>It bears 12 letters in escutcheons spaced 30&deg; apart, along with the number Tradition assigns to each. Moreover, each escutcheon bears a distinct color or luminous ray.</p>

<p>The homology of these colors in opposing pairs at 180&deg; reconstitutes the dual white ray or diameter seen in the small central circle.</p>

<h5>Planetary Crown of the Utterance</h5>

<p>The third zone, with a bluish ground, is the Planetary Array of the Utterance.</p>

<p>It consists of four equilateral triangles dividing the Planisphere into twelve equal parts.</p>

<p>Two of these triangles&mdash;vertically homologous through their north and south angles&mdash;form the Star of the Hexagon, which Antiquity assigns to the metrology of the circle. For indeed, the hexagon&rsquo;s side equals its radius.</p>

<p>This is the Star of the Solstices of the Word, the Barashitha or sixfold Utterance of ancient cosmogonies. These four triangles generate among themselves, through intersection, three interferential squares whose sides are also equal to the radius.</p>

<p>The angles of these four trigones bear letters, each carrying its number and a specific color&mdash;and moreover, an interferential color generated by overlap at each square's angle.</p>

<h5>Triangle of the Word Jesus</h5>

<p><i>Land of the Living</i></p>

<p>The fundamental Triangle, morphological equivalent of the number 3, divides the circumference into three equal 120&deg; parts. It establishes the figure's vertical axis and fixes its north or zenith at its apex.</p>

<p>Its name writes itself through the identity of its three zodiacal letters with corresponding astral signs, of which they are the prototypes. This same identity is further completed by other non-escutcheoned solar alphabets.</p>

<p>This name is <i>Y-Pho</i>, Word of God, and the term <i>PhO</i> simultaneously signifies: Utterance, voice, sound, and light.</p>

<p>But upon governing the astral Circles, this same fundamental Trigone assigns to its apex the planetary letter forming a small equilateral triangle with a bisector&mdash;representing the celestial poles' North-South axis&mdash;which finds its true vertical position only there.</p>

<p>Its name then becomes <i>Y-Sho</i>: <i>JeShU</i>.</p>

<h5>Triangle of Mary</h5>

<p><i>Sea of Living Waters:</i></p>

<p>The trigone homologous to the preceding has its apex at the South. It reads: <i>MaRiaH</i>, following the rules of the harmonic or euphonic table from Valmiki's Ramayana regarding the descending letter R: Ri.</p>

<p>The hexagonal star it forms with the prior divides the circumference into six equal 60&deg; parts.</p>

<h5>Solstitial Star of the Word</h5>

<p>These first two triangles in hexagonal star formation are: the cosmogonic Barashitha, the sixfold creative Utterance, the hexagonal Principle of the six days of creation.</p>

<p>Ancient Tradition names the first trigone: "Divine Earth" and the second: "Living Water."</p>

<h5>Triangle of the Ether</h5>

<p>The third triangle has its apex at the West. It reads: "<i>LaKaZa</i>", the Ether, the Etheric Power.</p>

<h5>Triangle of the Divine Fire</h5>

<p>The fourth triangle has its apex at the East.</p>

<p>It reads: <i>HouT</i>. The Divine Fire.</p>

<h5>Star of the Equinoxes of the Word</h5>

<p><i>of His Angels or ALaHIM:</i></p>

<p>The third and fourth trigones united form the Star of the Equinoxes of the Word. Read collectively from the Center A, westward to La, then eastward to H, <i>ALaH</i>, a pronoun meaning: "That One."</p>

<p>The Arabs, doubling the letter L through their Lam-Alif, pronounce this pronoun <i>ALLaH</i>. This same hexagonal Star of the Equinoxes of the Word, referenced to the first two letters of the Solstitial Star, reads: <i>ALaH-IM</i>, and inversely <i>MI-HeLa</i>.</p>

<p>It is instrumental to the Barashitha, and forms with it the dodecagonal Star of the Word, dividing the Circle into twelve 30&deg; parts.</p>

<h5>Cosmological Musical Crown</h5>

<p>Following the three prior zones or crowns comes a musical crown composed of a double diatonic hexad, where each note corresponds to the color, number, and letter of the same degree or decan in the aforementioned zones or crowns.</p>

<p>The Archeometer presents seven diatonic modes. The fundamental emissive note is Sol, corresponding to the letter Y and the color blue.</p>

<p>But once the circle is defined by the four equilateral triangles, its octave alone speaks planetarily, and the gravest note then becomes "<i>Si</i>", the major third of the original "<i>Sol</i>".</p>

<p>Historical antiquity wholly lost the precise notion of the fundamental "<i>Sol</i>" of the Virgin's Lyre. It retained more or less clearly only that of the fundamental "<i>Si</i>" which, from the Archeometer's apex to base, yields the seventh chord "<i>Si-la</i>" divided into two conjunct fourths by the note at the figure's solar center: <i>Mi</i>.</p>

<p>"<i>Si-mi-la</i>" reads on the Archeometer: <i>ShNaH</i>, THE YEAR, and inversely South-North: <i>NaHaSh</i>: the Serpent.</p>

<p>Measured by conjunct thirds rather than fourths, this chord "<i>Si-r&eacute;-fa-la</i>" is the interferential or direct enharmonic of the diatonic musical equivalent of the interferential or diametric North-South axis.</p>

<p>The sonometry of the numbers 10, 80, 6 yields upon the fundamental string of <i>Sol</i> the archeometric gamut of 22 intervals.</p>

<p>Just as the numbers of the letters form a qualitative arithmology&mdash;experimental through sound&mdash;so too they determine a qualitative morphology, identically experimental, through harmonic intervals upon the string at rest or in vibration.</p>

<p>From this results an archeometric morphological alphabet of 22 forms.</p>

<h5>Astral Zodiacal Crown</h5>

<p>The astral zodiacal crown occupies an orange-pink tinted zone, with twelve escutcheons bearing the traditional signs of the Zodiac. The word "Zodiac" derives from inverted Sanskrit: <i>Kya-Devas</i> or <i>Kaya-Devas</i>&mdash;the Path of Angels.</p>

<h5>Astral Planetary Crown</h5>

<p>This is the bluish zone of diatonic planets in double hexad, marked with their traditional signs in diurnal and nocturnal positions, with the Sun at the Center, represented by the note <i>Mi</i>.</p>

<p>These signs&mdash;astral, zodiacal, planetary, and solar&mdash;are derived from morphological letters employed on the escutcheons and the angles of the four inscribed trigones.</p>

<p>Thus, these same letters, being self-positioned and not arbitrarily placed on the Archeometer, speak autologically therein&mdash;without human will introducing any caprice into this direct Utterance, provided its laws are respected.</p>

<p>This Utterance is translatable into the most ancient tongues called in Sanskrit: "Languages of the City or of Divine Civilization: <i>Devanagari</i>," as opposed to other non-archeometric or non-archeometered languages termed <i>Prakrits</i> or of Savage Civilization.</p>

<h5>Dodecagonal Crown of Chromic Circum-Solar Rays</h5>

<p>Following these six zones or crowns comes a luminous reproduction of the World of Glory&mdash;a double hexagonal star dominated by the luminous trigone (blue, yellow, and red) corresponding to the Principle-Trinity and the three letters of its name.</p>

<p><i>I-PhO</i>, Word of God; <i>I-ShO</i>, JeShU.</p>

<h5>Crown of White Rays</h5>

<p>This reproduction of the World of Glory surrounds the final indigo-tinted zone&mdash;the musical staff of five lines where the six diameters or twelve white rays intersect, homologizing complementary colors.</p>

<h5>Solar Center</h5>

<p>And finally, the note <i>Mi</i> representing the central Sun, which&mdash;with the semicircle above it&mdash;forms the letter <i>Na</i>; just as the horizontal white ray forms the morphological letter A.</p>

<h4>Summary of the Brief Description</h4>

<h5>Dual Crown of 360 Degrees: Boundless Time, Eternity</h5>

<p>The zone of degrees or differential homological protractor corresponds hermeneutically to Eternity or Boundless Time. The homological reproduction of 360 through addition at each degree demonstrates experimentally the omnipresence of God.</p>

<p>3 signifies the Word, 6 the Holy Spirit, and zero nothing by itself; yet preceded by these two digits, nothing becomes All&mdash;that is, the defined Universe: 360.</p>

<h5>Eternal World of Glory</h5>

<p>From the degree zone to the musical notes, there are four hierarchical intervals constituting what Tradition calls "The Glory"&mdash;the incorruptible Creation of the Word, His eternal Kingdom and that of His immortal Powers.</p>

<h5>Temporal World of the Astral Heavens</h5>

<p>The next two zones or crowns represent the sidereal Heaven&mdash;the temporal&mdash;in its type of harmony and organism determined by the prototype or archetype above.</p>
