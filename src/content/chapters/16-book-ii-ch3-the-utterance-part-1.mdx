<h4>The Archeometric Planisphere</h4>

<p><i>An organic, harmonic, and symmetrical instrument, founded upon the combination of several Zones of Circles, Radii, and concentric Polygons capable of evolving around a common central point.</i></p>

<p>The said Zones comprise:</p>

<p>1° A dual Circle of <b>360°</b> each evolving in opposite directions, such that each degree yields two numbers, their sum always being <b>360</b>, and their dextrogyral or levogyral inversion permitting easy metrology in both directions;</p>

<p><b>2° A fixed</b> duodenary Zone called <b>Zodiac of modal Letters</b>. It is divided into equal parts, each of <b>30°</b>, further divisible into minutes and seconds. Each twelfth of this <b>Zone</b> contains, at its center, its morphological letter and the number of that letter within an Escutcheon bearing a precisely corresponding special color;</p>

<p><b>Total: XII morphological Letters, XII arithmological Numbers, each specific to one of them, XII Colors;</b></p>

<p><b>3° A mobile</b> Zone called <b>planetary of Letters</b>.</p>

<p>This is formed by <b>XII Angles</b> of <b>IV equilateral Triangles</b> intersecting regularly. Each of the <b>XII Angles</b> is tangent to one of the <b>Escutcheons</b> of the preceding <b>Zone</b> and bears a morphological Letter, the arithmological Number proper to this Letter, the Color of the Escutcheon to which it is tangent, a musical staff, and a Note.</p>

<p><b>Total: XII Angles, XII Letters, XII Numbers, XII Colors, XII Notes;</b></p>

<p><b>4° A fixed</b> astral Zodiacal Zone, with its <b>XII Signs</b> derived from the <b>XII Zodiacal Letters</b>. Each Sign in its Escutcheon bearing the Color of its Correspondence with the <b>Zones</b> above;</p>

<p><b>5° A mobile</b> astral planetary Zone, with its <b>VII astral diatonic Signs</b> and all their Correspondences, each appearing in its proper Domiciles both Diurnal and Nocturnal, and bearing the Color of the planetary Letter from which its morphology derives.</p>

<p><b>Total: XII planetary Signs, of which V are repeated = VII, and XII Colors;</b></p>

<p><b>6° A Zone</b> of <b>XII angles</b> of <b>IV equilateral Triangles</b>, intersecting regularly beneath the <b>generative and metrological Triangle</b>, corresponding, by its angles, to the <b>Sign of Virgo</b>, the <b>Sign of Capricorn</b>, and the <b>Sign of Taurus</b>. Each Angle bears the Colors of the Sign to which it corresponds.</p>

<p><b>Total: XII Colors, XII Angles, IV Triangles including one generative, two hexagons or two Pairs of equilateral Tangent Triangles, one Pair solstitial by its North-South Angles, the other equinoctial by its East-West Angles, all Angles of each Pair spaced apart by the length of the Radius of their tangent Circle;</b></p>

<p><b>7° A central Circle</b> containing a <b>Musical Staff</b>, a <b>Note</b> at the Common Center, a morphological <b>Letter</b> on this Note, <b>XII white rays</b> forming <b>VI white diameters</b>, passing through the Center, one North-South, the other East-West, and all at <b>30°</b> from each other on the circle.</p>

<p>The organic and harmonic <b>planisphere</b> has its Poles and Equator of Correspondences, its year, its seasons, its left-right symmetry of organicities and harmonicities either direct or interferential. It speaks precisely through all its concordant elements and through all their combinations.</p>

<p>The analysis of its synthesis and the laws that presided over its composition will be given after its detailed description.</p>
